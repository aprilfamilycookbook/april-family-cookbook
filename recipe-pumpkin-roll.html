Pumpkin Roll

From the kitchen of Mary Ringlein
← Back to All Recipes

The pumpkin roll became a Thanksgiving tradition across America in the 1970s and ’80s, helped along by Libby’s, whose canned pumpkin labels printed a version of this recipe that spread from kitchen to kitchen through holiday seasons. The technique — a thin spiced cake baked in a jelly roll pan, turned out hot onto a powdered-sugar cloth and rolled up immediately to cool — is the same one home bakers have used for generations. Rolling while hot is the key: it trains the warm, pliable cake into shape before it can crack. Mary Ringlein’s version is generously spiced with cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg, and the cream cheese filling is rich and straightforward. It can be made ahead, refrigerated for hours, or frozen — which makes it one of the most practical holiday desserts in the book.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1 c sugar
  • ⅔ c pumpkin
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • ¾ c flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ginger
  • ½ tsp nutmeg
  • Optional: chopped nuts for topping
  • Filling:
  • 8 oz. cream cheese, softened
  • 4 Tbsp butter, softened
  • 1 c powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°. Grease a 10×15×1-inch jelly roll pan.
  2. Beat eggs at high speed for 5 minutes. Gradually add sugar and beat well.
  3. Add pumpkin and lemon juice; mix well.
  4. Add flour, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, ginger, and nutmeg; mix until combined.
  5. Spread batter into prepared pan. Sprinkle with chopped nuts if desired.
  6. Bake at 375° for 10–15 minutes.
  7. Immediately turn cake out onto a clean cloth dusted generously with powdered sugar. Roll up the cake in the cloth while still hot — this prevents cracking. Cool 1 hour.
  8. Meanwhile, beat cream cheese, butter, powdered sugar, and vanilla until smooth.
  9. Unroll cooled cake and spread filling evenly over the surface.
  10. Re-roll loosely without the cloth. Refrigerate for 2 hours or more before serving. Can be frozen.
