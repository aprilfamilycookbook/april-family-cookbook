Foolproof Fudge

From the kitchen of Terri Board
← Back to All Recipes

This is the Eagle Brand recipe — the one that has appeared on cans of sweetened condensed milk for generations and launched countless batches of holiday fudge across American kitchens. Eagle Brand itself dates to 1856, when Gail Borden developed condensed milk as a shelf-stable food source, but it was in the 1950s and ’60s that the company began printing this simple fudge formula on its labels, and home cooks never looked back. The genius of it is the condensed milk: it acts as both sweetener and thickening agent, eliminating the need for a candy thermometer or any of the tricky temperature work that makes traditional fudge so unpredictable. Melt the chips with the milk and salt over low heat, stir in vanilla and nuts, spread in the pan, chill two hours. That’s it. Makes 2 pounds. Terri Board’s version is faithful to the original — and faithful to the reason it earned the name.

Ingredients

  • 3 c (18 oz.) semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 (14 oz.) can sweetened condensed milk
  • Dash of salt
  • ½–1 c nuts, chopped
  • 1½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Line an 8 or 9-inch square pan with foil. Butter the foil.
  2. In a heavy saucepan over low heat, melt the chocolate chips with the sweetened condensed milk and salt, stirring frequently. Do not rush this step — keep the heat low to prevent the chocolate from seizing or scorching.
  3. Remove from heat as soon as the chips are fully melted and the mixture is smooth.
  4. Stir in the chopped nuts and vanilla extract.
  5. Spread evenly in the prepared pan.
  6. Chill for 2 hours, or until firm.
  7. Lift the fudge out of the pan using the foil, peel off the foil, and cut into squares.
  8. Store covered in the refrigerator. Makes approximately 2 lbs.
