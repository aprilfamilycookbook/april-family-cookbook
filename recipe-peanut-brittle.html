Peanut Brittle

By Patty Hykes

Peanut brittle is one of the oldest American confections — a Southern invention rooted in the nut-and-syrup traditions of the 19th century, made famous by home candy-makers who cooked sugar to the hard crack stage and folded in roasted peanuts before it set. Patty Hykes’ version takes the shortcut that changed holiday candy-making in the 1980s: the microwave. The sugar, corn syrup, and raw peanuts cook together in a casserole dish on high, then the real magic happens at the end — baking soda, margarine, and vanilla are stirred in all at once. The baking soda reacts with the hot syrup instantly, foaming and aerating it into the light, porous, shatter-crisp texture that defines good brittle. You have to work fast at that point. Spread it as thin as possible on a greased sheet, let it cool, and break it into shards.

Ingredients

  • 1 c sugar
  • ½ c light corn syrup
  • Dash of salt
  • ¾ c shelled raw peanuts
  • 1 Tbsp margarine
  • 1½ tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp vanilla

Instructions

  1. Spray a large baking sheet generously with cooking spray and set aside.
  2. In a 3-quart microwave-safe casserole dish, combine the sugar, corn syrup, and salt. Stir in the raw peanuts.
  3. Microwave on HIGH for 8–10 minutes, stirring once or twice during cooking, until the mixture is light golden brown. Watch carefully — it can darken quickly near the end.
  4. Remove from the microwave. Working quickly, stir in the margarine, baking soda, and vanilla all at once. The mixture will foam and puff up — stir just until light and fluffy.
  5. Immediately pour onto the prepared baking sheet. Spread as thin as possible using a greased spatula or the back of a spoon. Speed matters here — the candy sets fast.
  6. Cool completely until hardened.
  7. Break into pieces and store in an airtight container at room temperature.

The baking soda moment: When you stir in the baking soda, it reacts with the hot syrup to create thousands of tiny bubbles — that’s what makes brittle light and crispy instead of rock-hard. Stir just until foamy and spread immediately. Don’t overwork it or the bubbles will collapse.

Microwave wattage: Timing varies by microwave. Start checking at 7 minutes. The brittle should be light golden brown — if it smells at all scorched, it’s gone too far.
