Peanut Butter Chocolate Cookies

By Mary Ringlein

A peanut butter cookie with unsweetened chocolate stirred right into the dough — and made with Splenda in place of granulated sugar, which makes it a lower-sugar option without sacrificing the rich, nutty flavor. The unsweetened chocolate (finely chopped, not chips) gets mixed in at the end so it stays in small pockets throughout the dough rather than fully incorporating, giving each cookie a slight bitterness that balances the sweetness of the brown sugar and peanut butter. The fork criss-cross finish is the same classic move as a traditional peanut butter cookie. Makes 48 cookies.

Ingredients

  • 1 c unsalted butter, softened
  • 1⅓ c Splenda Granular
  • ⅔ c dark brown sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 c flour
  • 1½ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 c creamy peanut butter
  • 2 oz unsweetened chocolate, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Place the butter, Splenda, brown sugar, and vanilla in a medium bowl. Using the paddle attachment of an electric mixer, beat until light and creamy, about 1½ minutes.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Add the flour, baking soda, and salt. Mix until well combined.
  5. Add the peanut butter and stir until fully incorporated.
  6. Add the finely chopped unsweetened chocolate and mix briefly — just until distributed.
  7. Roll the dough into 48 balls using approximately 1 tablespoon of dough per ball.
  8. Place on an ungreased cookie sheet.
  9. Dip a fork in water and press a criss-cross pattern into the top of each cookie to flatten slightly.
  10. Bake at 350°F for 10–15 minutes, until set.
  11. Cool on the pan for 2–3 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

About Splenda: This recipe uses Splenda Granular in place of white sugar for a lower-sugar cookie. Splenda Granular substitutes 1:1 by volume, so if you prefer to use regular sugar, replace the 1⅓ c Splenda with 1⅓ c granulated sugar — no other changes needed. The dark brown sugar stays as-is either way.
