5 Bean Hot Dish

From the kitchen of Bob April

The 'hot dish' — a term used more in Minnesota and the Dakotas than in Ohio, though the concept is universal — is the Midwestern church potluck in a single pan. Five kinds of beans, ground meat, and a sweet-savory sauce designed to feed a crowd and hold well on a buffet table: this is community cooking in its most honest form. Its name may even reflect Bob April's North Dakota years.

← Back to All Recipes

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. ground meat, browned
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green pepper, chopped
  • 1 can kidney beans
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 can lima beans
  • 1 can pinto beans
  • 1 can navy beans
  • 12 oz. tomato paste
  • 2 Tbsp chili powder
  • 2 (15 oz.) cans diced tomatoes
  • 16 oz. V-8 juice
  • ½ c brown sugar
  • ⅛ c molasses
  • 4 strips bacon, cooked and crumbled

Instructions

  1. Combine all ingredients in a crock pot and cook on low for 8 hours.
💡 Tip: Great for potlucks — feeds a crowd and gets better as it sits.
