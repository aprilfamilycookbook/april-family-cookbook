Buckeyes

By Mary April

← All Recipes

Buckeyes are an Ohio tradition — peanut butter balls dipped in chocolate, left with a circle of peanut butter showing on top to resemble the nut of the Ohio buckeye tree. Mary April’s version uses paraffin wax melted into the chocolate for a glossy, firm coating that sets cleanly. Each ball gets dipped three times. Rich, sweet, and unmistakably Ohio.

Ingredients

  • 5½ c powdered sugar
  • 2 c peanut butter
  • ½ lb (2 sticks) butter, melted
  • 1½ tsp vanilla
  • 12 oz semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • ¼ lb paraffin wax (food-grade)

Instructions

  1. Mix together the powdered sugar, peanut butter, melted butter, and vanilla until smooth.
  2. Roll the mixture into 1-inch balls. Place on a waxed paper-lined baking sheet.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes until firm.
  4. In a double boiler over simmering water, melt the chocolate chips and paraffin wax together, stirring until smooth.
  5. Using a toothpick or dipping fork, dip each ball into the chocolate 3 times, letting excess drip off.
  6. Place on waxed paper to set.
  7. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

About paraffin wax: Food-grade paraffin (Gulf Wax) gives the chocolate coating a glossy finish and firm set. Find it in the canning aisle. Substitute 1 tablespoon vegetable shortening for a slightly softer coating.
