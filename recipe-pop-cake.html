Pop Cake

By Mary Ringlein

Pop Cake is the kind of recipe that works because it shouldn’t. Replacing the oil, eggs, and water in a boxed cake mix with a can of soda produces a cake that’s surprisingly light and moist, with a subtle flavor from the pop that plays off whichever mix you choose. The frosting is even simpler — Cool Whip stirred together with a packet of dry Jell-O mix, which gives it color, flavor, and a little body without any cooking or powdered sugar. Mary Ringlein offers two starting points: yellow cake with orange pop and orange Jell-O, or yellow cake with red pop and strawberry Jell-O. From there the combinations are wide open — lemon cake with lemon-lime pop, chocolate cake with cherry pop, white cake with grape. Make it your own.

Ingredients

  • 1 box cake mix (any flavor)
  • 1 (12 oz.) can flavored soda pop
  • 1 (8 oz.) container Cool Whip
  • 1 pkg sugar-free Jell-O mix (any flavor, to match or complement the pop)

Instructions

  1. Mix the cake mix and soda pop together until combined. Do not add the eggs, oil, or water called for on the cake box — the pop replaces them.
  2. Pour into a greased pan and bake according to the directions on the cake mix box.
  3. Cool completely.
  4. Make the frosting: Stir together the Cool Whip and dry Jell-O mix (do not prepare the Jell-O — use the powder straight from the packet) until smooth and fluffy.
  5. Spread the frosting over the cooled cake.
  6. Refrigerate until ready to serve.

Mary Ringlein’s favorite combinations:

  • Yellow cake + orange pop + orange Jell-O
  • Yellow cake + red pop (like Fanta or Crush) + strawberry Jell-O

The rule of thumb: match the pop and Jell-O flavors to each other, and choose a cake mix that complements both. Lemon cake + lemon-lime pop + lemon Jell-O is another winner.
