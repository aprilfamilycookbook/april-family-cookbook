Peanut Butter Blossoms

By Mary April

Peanut Butter Blossoms became an American cookie jar classic after first appearing in a Pillsbury Bake-Off in 1957, when Freda Smith of Gibsonburg, Ohio entered them and nearly won. The combination of a soft peanut butter cookie rolled in sugar with a Hershey’s Kiss pressed into the center while still warm has been a holiday staple ever since — simple to make, instantly recognizable, and just the right balance of sweet and salty. Mary April’s recipe follows the classic formula: cream butter and peanut butter, add both sugars, mix in the dry ingredients, roll in sugar, and bake. The Kiss goes on after the first bake, then the cookies go back in just long enough to melt it slightly into place.

Ingredients

  • 1¾ c flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ c butter, softened
  • ½ c peanut butter
  • ½ c sugar
  • ½ c brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • Extra sugar, for rolling
  • Hershey’s Kisses (1 per cookie), unwrapped

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Sift together the flour, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and peanut butter until smooth.
  4. Gradually add the sugar and brown sugar, creaming well.
  5. Add the egg and vanilla and mix until combined.
  6. Gradually add the dry ingredients, mixing well until a soft dough forms.
  7. Shape the dough into balls using a rounded teaspoon of dough per cookie.
  8. Roll each ball in sugar and place on an ungreased cookie sheet.
  9. Bake at 375°F for 8 minutes.
  10. Remove from oven and immediately press one unwrapped Hershey’s Kiss firmly into the center of each cookie — the cookie will crack slightly around the edges.
  11. Return to the oven and bake for 2–5 minutes more.
  12. Cool on the pan for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Freda Smith of Gibsonburg, Ohio nearly won the 1957 Pillsbury Bake-Off with this recipe — a point of Ohio pride worth knowing. Unwrap all the Kisses before you start baking so you can press them in quickly while the cookies are still hot.
