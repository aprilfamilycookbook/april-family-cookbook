Perfect Apple Pie

By Mary Ringlein

Apple pie is as close to a universal American dessert as exists — tart apples, warm spice, a flaky crust, and just enough sugar to let the fruit shine. Mary Ringlein’s recipe earns its name with a few deliberate choices: tart apples (which hold their shape and balance the sugar), a flour-thickened filling that sets cleanly without going gluey, and a hot 400° oven that drives the steam out fast and crisps the crust before the filling has a chance to make it soggy. The sugar sprinkled on top before baking is the finishing touch — a sparkle of sweetness that catches the heat and caramelizes into something better than frosting. Serve warm.

Ingredients

  • 6–7 tart apples, pared and sliced thin
  • ¾–1 c sugar
  • 2 Tbsp flour
  • ½–1 tsp cinnamon
  • Dash of nutmeg
  • Dash of salt
  • Pastry for a 2-crust 9-inch pie
  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • Additional sugar, for sprinkling on top crust

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
  2. Pare the apples and slice thin.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Add the sliced apples and toss to coat.
  4. Line a 9-inch pie plate with the bottom pastry crust.
  5. Fill with the apple mixture. Dot the top of the filling with butter.
  6. Adjust the top crust over the filling. Trim and flute the edges to seal.
  7. Sprinkle the top crust with sugar for sparkle.
  8. Bake at 400°F for 50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  9. Cool at least 1 hour before slicing to allow the filling to set.

For the crust: See Mary Ringlein’s Pie Crust for a from-scratch pastry — her two-stage shortening technique is perfect for a two-crust pie like this one.

Best apples: Granny Smith, Jonathan, Cortland, or Braeburn hold their shape and give the filling real tartness. Avoid Red Delicious — they turn mushy.
