Pecan Pie Bars

From the kitchen of Terri Board
← Back to All Recipes

Pecan pie has deep roots in the American South — the earliest recipes emerged from Texas in the 1920s, and when Karo syrup printed a copy on their corn syrup bottle in the mid-1920s, it became popular across the country. Pecan pie bars brought those same flavors into a more practical, party-friendly format that spread through community cookbooks and church potluck collections from the 1970s onward. Terri Board’s version uses sweetened condensed milk and a bright hit of RealLemon juice in the filling instead of the traditional corn syrup — a combination that gives the bars a slightly lighter, tangier character than classic pecan pie while keeping that rich, gooey filling everyone loves. The shortbread-style crust comes together in minutes and the whole pan cuts into neat, portable bars. Perfect for holiday cookie trays and potlucks.

Ingredients

  • 2 c flour
  • ¼ c brown sugar
  • ½ c butter, cold
  • 1½ c chopped pecans
  • 1 (14 oz.) can sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 2 Tbsp RealLemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°.
  2. In a medium bowl, combine flour and brown sugar. Cut in cold butter until mixture is crumbly.
  3. Press mixture firmly onto the bottom of an ungreased 13×9-inch baking pan.
  4. Bake 15 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, combine pecans, sweetened condensed milk, beaten eggs, and RealLemon juice. Mix well.
  6. Pour filling over the hot crust and spread evenly.
  7. Bake 25 minutes, or until filling is set.
  8. Cool completely before cutting into bars. Store covered at room temperature.
