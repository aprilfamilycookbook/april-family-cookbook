Peanut Butter Cookies

By Mary April

The classic peanut butter cookie — soft, rich, and marked with the fork criss-cross that has been the signature of this cookie since at least the 1930s, when peanut butter first started appearing in American baking in earnest. The cross-hatch isn’t just decorative; pressing the dough flat with a fork ensures even baking for a cookie that holds together without being hard. Mary April’s version uses both white and brown sugar for depth, sifted flour for a tender crumb, and calls for chilling the dough before rolling — the step that keeps the balls from spreading too thin in the oven. Simple, dependable, and exactly what a peanut butter cookie should be.

Ingredients

  • ½ c butter, softened
  • ½ c peanut butter
  • ½ c sugar
  • ½ c brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1¼ c sifted flour
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • ¾ tsp baking soda
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the butter, peanut butter, sugar, brown sugar, and egg until smooth and well combined.
  2. Sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients and mix until a soft dough forms.
  4. Chill the dough in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F. Lightly grease a baking sheet.
  6. Roll the chilled dough into balls about the size of walnuts.
  7. Place 3 inches apart on the prepared baking sheet.
  8. Flatten each ball with a fork dipped in flour, pressing a criss-cross pattern into the top.
  9. Bake at 375°F for 10–12 minutes, until just set. Do not overbake — they will firm up as they cool.
  10. Cool on the pan for 2–3 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.
