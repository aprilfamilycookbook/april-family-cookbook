Sunday Pot Roast

📍 Contributed by Grandma April · Est. tradition since 1962

This was the recipe that meant Sunday. No matter what else was happening in the house — this roast meant everyone would find their way back to the table. Grandma April made it every Sunday she hosted for over 40 years. The secret, she always said, was patience and a good cast iron pot.

Prep Time20 min
Cook Time3 hrs
Total3 hr 20 min
Serves8
DifficultyEasy
← Back to Recipes

🛒 Ingredients

  • 3–4 lb chuck roast
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 4 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 4 medium potatoes, quartered
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
✏️ Grandma's Tip

Don't lift the lid during cooking — every peek adds 15 minutes to your cook time. Trust the process.

👩‍🍳 Instructions

  1. 1
    Season the Roast

    Pat the roast dry with paper towels. Mix the salt, pepper, and garlic powder together and rub generously all over the roast. Let it sit at room temperature for 15 minutes while you prep the vegetables.

  2. 2
    Sear for Flavor

    Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or cast iron pot over medium-high heat until it shimmers. Sear the roast on all sides, about 3–4 minutes per side, until a deep brown crust forms. Don't rush this step — the crust is where all the flavor lives.

  3. 3
    Build the Base

    Remove the roast and set aside. In the same pot, add the onions and smashed garlic. Cook for 2–3 minutes, stirring up all the brown bits from the bottom of the pan. Add the beef broth and cream of mushroom soup, stirring until combined.

  4. 4
    Add Vegetables & Roast

    Nestle the roast back in the pot. Surround it with the carrots and potatoes. Lay the rosemary and thyme sprigs on top. The liquid should come about halfway up the roast — add a splash more broth if needed.

  5. 5
    Low & Slow

    Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. Cook in the oven at 300°F (150°C) for 3 to 3.5 hours, until the meat is fork-tender and nearly falling apart. If cooking on the stovetop, keep at the lowest simmer with the lid on tight.

  6. 6
    Rest & Serve

    Remove from heat and let rest, covered, for 15 minutes. Pull the meat apart with two forks. Serve with the vegetables and ladle the rich pan gravy over everything. Good bread is not optional.

📝 Notes & Variations

This works beautifully in a slow cooker — sear the meat first, then cook on LOW for 8 hours. For extra richness, replace ½ cup of the broth with red wine. Leftovers (if any) make incredible sandwiches the next day.
